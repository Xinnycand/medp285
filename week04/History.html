The history of music is vast, stretching back tens of thousands of years, and can be understood in terms of periods of development across cultures.



Musical education has evolved over time, playing a critical role in how cultures develop, preserve, and pass down musical knowledge and skills. Throughout history, conservatories and music academies began to spread throughout the world and had religious, social, and cultural relations in addition to it's education.



Music has always been a source of entertainment. It plays a central role in celebrations, dances, and festivals as it has provided people with a means of leisure, relaxation, and joy.



Throughout history, music has shifted from being a privilege of the elite or religious communities to a more inclusive community. It is used, avalible, and loved by people of all ages and backgrounds. Music has helped shape not only the development of musical traditions but also the intellectual, social, and cultural fabric of societies.
